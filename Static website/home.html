Simplifying Academic Management

Elevenrollment streamlines the academic experience by providing easy access to course registration, academic calendars, and tuition payment, all in one convenient platform.

Our Vision

Elevenrollment aims to simplify the course enrollment process, provide easy access to finance account information, and enable students to track their academic progress with ease. Our app is designed to be user-friendly, intuitive, and reliable, helping students stay organized and focused on their academic goals.

Sign Up / Sign In

Registration

This feature allows students to add the course if they want to register to it. In case students change their mind, then they can drop that course too. This feature help students to make their enrollment process smooth.

E-forms

This feature helps students of our app to find all their important forms related to university under one roof. They can also request for some specific forms from their university authorities under this tab too.

Academic Progress

This feature allows students to keep track of information about their academic history, including GPA, transcripts, courses taken, academic achievements, and test scores.

OUR INTENDED USERS

Designed for students of all levels - simplify your academic journey!

The intended users of our program are students of universities and colleges who want to manage their academic and financial information in one place. This app is especially useful for students who want to add or drop courses, check their finance account summaries, track their academic progress, access important e-forms, and get answers to frequently asked questions (FAQs). The app is designed to be user-friendly and accessible to students of all levels, from freshmen to graduate students.

Reflections & Improvements

I. Introduction

The software engineering group project was a valuable learning experience for the team. In this retrospective, we will reflect on various aspects of the project, including communication, role assignments, and iteration planning. We will also discuss how our team's velocity changed over time and its impact on iteration planning. Finally, we will reflect on what we would do differently if we had the chance to start the project again

II. Communication

What went wrong and right in development

At the start of the project, our team had weak communication, which affected our progress. We were not clear about each other's roles and responsibilities, which led to confusion and delays. However, as we progressed through the project, we communicated more effectively. We scheduled regular meetings to discuss our progress and any issues that arose. We also used collaboration tools such as Telegram and Notion to keep track of our tasks and deadlines. Overall, our communication improved significantly over time, which helped us to work more efficiently.

III. Iteration Planning

What would we do differently, if we had the chance to start over?

At the beginning of the project, our team assigned roles for each iteration without first discussing our individual strengths and weaknesses. In hindsight, we realized that it would have been more effective to establish each team member's skills and experience before assigning roles for the entire project. By doing so, we could have assigned more substantial roles that played to each team member's strengths, rather than dividing tasks on a per-iteration basis. This approach would have allowed us to work more efficiently and deliver a higher-quality product.

IV. Changes in velocity

Are we using any specific techniques covered in the course (TDD, pair programming, scrums, etc)?

Our team's velocity changed significantly over time. Initially, we struggled to meet our targets, which affected our iteration planning. However, as we improved our communication and became more familiar with the project, our velocity increased. We were able to complete tasks more efficiently, which allowed us to plan our iterations more effectively. We also recognized the importance of adjusting our iteration plans as necessary to accommodate any changes in our velocity.

V. Conclusion

Overall, our software engineering group project was a valuable learning experience for our team. We learned the importance of effective communication, role assignments, and iteration planning. If we had the chance to start the project over again, we would have placed more emphasis on communication from the start. We would also have tried to establish clearer and more specific goals to help us focus our efforts. Additionally, we would have paid closer attention to our velocity and adjusted our iteration plans accordingly.
